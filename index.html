Rafael Nadal

Hard work pays off

  • Rafael Nadal Parera was born on 3 June 1986 in Manacor, a town on the island of Mallorca in the Balearic Islands, Spain.
  • Rafael Nadal is a Spanish professional tennis player. He is ranked world No. 4 in singles by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP) and has been ranked world No. 1 for 209 weeks, finishing as the year-end No. 1 five times.
  • Nadal has won 21 Grand Slam men's singles titles, the most in history, including a record 13 French Open titles.
  • He has won 91 ATP singles titles, with 62 of these on clay. Nadal's 81 consecutive wins on clay is the longest single-surface win streak in the Open Era.
  • Nadal was one of the most successful teenagers in ATP Tour history, reaching No. 2 in the world and winning 16 titles before his 20th birthday, including his first French Open and six Masters events.
  • After defeating Novak Djokovic at the 2010 US Open final, then 24-year-old Nadal became the youngest man in the Open Era to achieve the career Grand Slam, and the first man to win three Majors on three different surfaces (hard, grass and clay) in the same year.
  • After being defeated in Australia and Miama in 2005, Nadal dominated the spring clay-court season winning 24 consecutive singles matches and breaking Andre Agassi's Open Era record of consecutive match wins for a male teenager.
